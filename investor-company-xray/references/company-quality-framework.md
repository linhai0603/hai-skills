# Company Quality Framework

Use this framework for `investor-company-xray`.

## Core Question

Before asking whether the stock is cheap, ask whether the company is a business worth understanding.

## Review Dimensions

### 1. Business Understanding

- What does the company sell?
- Who pays, why do they pay, and how often?
- Is the business model simple enough for the user to explain?
- Is revenue driven by durable demand, one-off events, subsidies, fashion, or cycles?

### 2. Values And Governance

- Does the company appear to respect law, facts, customers, employees, and minority shareholders?
- Are controlling shareholders aligned with minority shareholders?
- Are incentives clear and healthy?
- Are there signs of related-party abuse, opaque governance, or repeated trust issues?

Integrity concerns are a red flag. If supplied evidence shows dishonest management, mark it as a severe concern.

### 3. Management Quality

- Is management focused, frugal, strategic, and operationally disciplined?
- Do words and actions match over time?
- Does founder or operator involvement improve accountability?
- Is the organization becoming bureaucratic or distracted?

### 4. Moat And Pricing Power

Look for evidence of durable competitive advantage:

- Pricing power.
- Brand strength.
- Network effects or ecosystem lock-in.
- Scale advantages.
- Distribution advantage.
- Switching costs.
- Patents, resources, licenses, or standards.
- Operational excellence that competitors struggle to copy.

Avoid vague moat claims. Ask what the customer cannot easily replace.

### 5. Product And Customer Reality

- Does the company make products or services customers genuinely need or strongly desire?
- Is customer stickiness proven by behavior, not slogans?
- Does the company listen to demand and execute well?

### 6. Financial Health

Use user-provided data only unless research is explicitly allowed.

Signals to review:

- ROE quality, not just level.
- Operating cash flow.
- Free cash flow.
- Debt burden and refinancing risk.
- Margins compared with peers.
- Revenue and profit durability.
- Dividend discipline when relevant.

High ROE can be distorted by leverage, accounting, or cycles. Pair it with cash flow, debt, and growth quality.

### 7. Industry And Time

- Is the industry expanding, stable, cyclical, or structurally pressured?
- Is the company trend exposure real, or only a story?
- Does industry growth translate into company advantage?
- Is the company positioned with the era, industry, leadership, and innovation?

Industry trend is not the same as company quality.

### 8. Valuation Caveat

A good company can still be a bad investment at the wrong price. If the user asks about investment action, hand off to a pre-trade check or valuation-specific review.

## Output Status

- `research candidate`: quality signals are strong enough to continue research.
- `watchlist only`: promising but evidence gaps remain.
- `quality concern`: serious issues need resolution.
- `out of circle`: not understandable enough for a responsible decision.
