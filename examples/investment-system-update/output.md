# Demo Output: Integrated Investment System

## 1. Price Discipline: Buy Good Assets At Good Prices

- A good company is not automatically a good investment.
- Price determines future return.
- Do not confuse business quality with investment attractiveness.
- There are no good stocks, only good prices.
- Waiting is part of investing. The market never lacks opportunities; it lacks patience.

## 2. Margin Of Safety: Buy Cheap Enough

- Margin of safety means buying cheap enough.
- Cheap enough does not mean statistically low valuation only.
- The price should give room for mistakes.

## 3. Risk Control

- Permanent loss of capital is the real risk.
- Position size should match conviction and downside.
- The biggest risk is pretending to understand what one does not understand.

## 4. Volatility: Understand Price Movement Correctly

- Volatility is not the same as risk.
- Many investors want the result but cannot endure the process.
- If a company compounds at a high rate, the stock can still fall sharply from time to time.

## What Changed

- The original price discipline section was preserved and strengthened.
- Margin of safety became its own section because the new notes gave it enough distinct content.
- Risk and volatility were separated so normal price movement is not confused with permanent capital loss.
- No outside concepts were added.
