# Position Review Framework

Use this framework for `investor-position-review`.

## Core Question

Do not ask "why did the price move?" first. Ask: does the original thesis still hold?

## Review Dimensions

### 1. Original Thesis

- Why was the position opened?
- What business, valuation, portfolio, or strategy logic supported it?
- Was the thesis written before purchase, or reconstructed after the fact?

### 2. What Changed

Classify changes:

- Price-only movement.
- Valuation change.
- Business performance change.
- Management or governance change.
- Industry or competitive change.
- Balance sheet or cash flow change.
- Personal cash need or risk tolerance change.

### 3. Noise, Volatility, Risk, Or Thesis Break

- `noise`: attention-grabbing but not relevant to the thesis.
- `normal volatility`: uncomfortable price movement without thesis damage.
- `risk`: a possible problem that needs monitoring.
- `thesis break`: evidence directly contradicts the original reason for owning.

### 4. Holding Discipline

Long-term holding is not passive stubbornness. It is conditional on:

- Business quality.
- Reasonable price or valuation discipline.
- Original thesis remaining valid.
- Position size staying within risk tolerance.
- No urgent cash need.

### 5. Position Risk

Even if the thesis is intact, review:

- Concentration.
- Leverage.
- Need for cash within 3-5 years.
- Emotional ability to tolerate volatility.
- Whether the position has grown beyond the intended allocation.

### 6. Decision Options

Do not choose for the user. Frame options:

- Continue monitoring.
- Gather missing evidence.
- Revisit position size.
- Update thesis.
- Escalate to a pre-trade check before any action.

## Review Status

- `thesis intact`: supplied evidence does not damage the original thesis.
- `watch closely`: important indicators are unclear or mixed.
- `thesis weakened`: one or more key assumptions are under pressure.
- `thesis broken`: supplied evidence contradicts the original thesis.
- `position risk issue`: allocation, funding, or emotional capacity is the main concern.
